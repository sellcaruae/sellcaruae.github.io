RTA Passing Before Selling Dubai – Complete Inspection Guide

By James Carter | Published April 1, 2026
Reading time: 11 minutes

A well-maintained car approaching the RTA inspection center signals to buyers that the vehicle meets all safety standards.

The RTA (Roads and Transport Authority) vehicle inspection is a critical step in the car selling process. A failed inspection can derail a sale at the last moment or cost you thousands in repairs. This guide covers what the RTA checks, common failure points, and how to prepare your car to pass on the first attempt.

What is the RTA Inspection?

The RTA inspection is a mandatory safety and emissions check required for all vehicle transfers in Dubai. The inspection covers mechanical condition, safety systems, emissions, and documentation. A passed inspection is required before the RTA will process the ownership transfer from seller to buyer.

The inspection typically takes 30–45 minutes and costs AED 100–150 depending on the vehicle type.

Common RTA Failure Points

Lights and indicators are among the first things RTA inspectors check. A blown bulb can cause a failed inspection.

Lights and Indicators

  • Headlights (both low and high beam)
  • Fog lights (if equipped)
  • Brake lights
  • Reverse lights
  • Indicators (turn signals)
  • Number plate lights

A single blown bulb will cause an RTA failure. Check all lights before your inspection appointment and replace any that are not functioning.

Brake System

  • Brake pad thickness (minimum 1.5mm required)
  • Brake fluid level
  • Brake responsiveness during test drive
  • Parking brake functionality

Worn brake pads are the second most common reason for RTA failures. If your brake pads are below 2mm, replace them before inspection.

Tyres and Suspension

  • Tyre tread depth (minimum 1.6mm required)
  • Tyre condition (no bulges, cuts, or damage)
  • Tyre pressure and balance
  • Suspension components (no leaks or damage)
  • Shock absorbers and springs

Tyres below 1.6mm tread depth will fail inspection. Replace worn tyres before your appointment.

Emissions and Engine

  • Emissions test (CO and HC levels)
  • Engine oil level and condition
  • Coolant level
  • No visible leaks
  • Engine bay cleanliness

Emissions failures are rare for well-maintained cars but can occur if your car has not been serviced recently. Ensure your engine oil is clean and at the correct level.

Safety Systems

  • Seatbelts (all seats)
  • Airbags (warning light check)
  • Horn functionality
  • Windscreen wipers
  • Mirrors and glass

Seatbelt failures are common. Test all seatbelts to ensure they lock and retract properly.

Pre-Inspection Checklist

A passed RTA inspection certificate is proof that your car meets all safety standards — a major selling point.

Complete this checklist 1 week before your RTA inspection appointment:

  • Lights: Test all headlights, fog lights, brake lights, reverse lights, indicators, and number plate lights. Replace any blown bulbs.
  • Brakes: Check brake pad thickness. If below 2mm, replace. Test brake responsiveness.
  • Tyres: Check tread depth on all four tyres. If below 1.6mm, replace. Check pressure and balance.
  • Fluids: Check engine oil level and condition. Top up if needed. Check coolant level.
  • Seatbelts: Test all seatbelts for proper locking and retraction.
  • Wipers: Replace worn wiper blades.
  • Horn: Test horn functionality.
  • Engine bay: Clean engine bay. Wipe down hoses and connections. Check for visible leaks.
  • Mirrors and glass: Ensure all mirrors are intact and glass is clear.
  • Warning lights: Clear any warning lights on the dashboard.

How to Schedule an RTA Inspection

You can schedule an RTA inspection online through the RTA website or mobile app, or visit an RTA inspection centre in person. Popular inspection centres in Dubai include Al Barsha, Al Qusais, and Jebel Ali.

Schedule your inspection 1–2 weeks before you intend to list your car. This gives time to address any issues if the car fails.

What Happens After Passing

Once your car passes the RTA inspection, you receive a passed inspection certificate (valid for 30 days). This certificate is proof that your car meets all safety standards and is a major selling point.

Include the RTA passed certificate in your listing. It signals to buyers that the car is mechanically sound and will pass their own inspection when they purchase.

If your car fails the inspection, you have 14 days to address the issues and retest. Retesting costs an additional AED 50–100.

RTA Inspection and Buyer Confidence

A passed RTA inspection before listing accelerates the sale process. Buyers know the car will pass their inspection, which removes a major concern. A car with a passed RTA certificate typically sells 20–30% faster than a car without one.

Some buyers will request that the seller obtain a passed RTA certificate before they commit to purchase. Having this certificate ready before listing can be a competitive advantage.

Key Takeaways

  • Schedule an RTA inspection 1–2 weeks before listing to address any issues.
  • Check all lights before inspection — a single blown bulb causes failure.
  • Replace worn brake pads and tyres — these are common failure points.
  • Clean your engine bay — it signals maintenance and care.
  • Include the passed RTA certificate in your listing — it accelerates the sale.
  • A passed RTA inspection typically increases buyer confidence and reduces negotiation.
